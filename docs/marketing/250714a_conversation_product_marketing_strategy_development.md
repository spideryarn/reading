# Assumptions - Research Brief: Scientific Journal Reviewer Pain Points & Product Positioning Validation

## Background & Context

**Product**: Spideryarn Reading - AI-assisted document analysis tool with intelligent highlighting and multi-color mapping capabilities

**Current Stage**: Early MVP with basic highlighting functionality, exploring product-market fit

**Core Hypothesis**: Scientific journal reviewers represent an underserved market segment that would pay for AI-assisted paper analysis tools that maintain human control while increasing efficiency.

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objective
Validate whether scientific journal reviewers experience sufficient pain points in their current workflow to justify purchasing an AI-assisted analysis tool.

### Secondary Objectives
1. Understand the actual reviewer workflow and identify high-friction moments
2. Test messaging hierarchy and value proposition resonance
3. Validate feature prioritization assumptions
4. Assess willingness to pay and pricing sensitivity

## Key Hypotheses to Test

### Hypothesis 1: Pain Point Validation
**Assumption**: Reviewers find their current paper analysis process time-consuming and frustrating, particularly:
- Hunting through papers for methodology issues
- Assessing novelty claims without full literature context
- Tracking evidence-to-conclusion connections

**Research Questions**:
- What specific tasks in the review process take the longest?
- Which parts of reviewing feel most tedious or frustrating?
- How do reviewers currently handle methodology assessment?
- What tools do they currently use (if any) for paper analysis?

### Hypothesis 2: AI Adoption Readiness
**Assumption**: Reviewers are open to AI assistance but concerned about maintaining control and judgment

**Research Questions**:
- Have they used AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for paper analysis?
- What are their primary concerns about AI-assisted reviewing?
- How important is "staying in control" vs. pure efficiency?
- What would make them trust an AI analysis tool?

### Hypothesis 3: Feature Value Ranking
**Assumption**: Intelligent highlighting with multi-color mapping is more valuable than generic AI summarization

**Research Questions**:
- How do they currently mark up or navigate papers?
- Would visual highlighting (color-coded by criteria) be useful?
- What's more valuable: time savings or thoroughness assurance?
- How important is staying within a single paper vs. literature comparison?

### Hypothesis 4: Messaging Resonance
**Assumption**: Efficiency messaging resonates most, but control messaging drives adoption

**Test Messages**:
- "Review papers 3x faster without missing critical details"
- "Stop hunting through papers for methodology issues"
- "AI-assisted paper review that keeps you in the driver's seat"

**Research Questions**:
- Which message would make them most likely to try the product?
- Which concerns would prevent them from adopting?
- How do they currently think about time spent on reviewing?

## Target Audience

### Primary Segment
**Active scientific journal reviewers** across disciplines, particularly:
- Academic researchers who review 6+ papers per year
- Editorial board members
- Senior researchers with 5+ years reviewing experience

### Secondary Segment
**Journal editors** who oversee the review process

### Screening Criteria
- Must have reviewed at least 3 papers in the last 12 months
- Must be willing to discuss their current workflow
- Mix of disciplines (but focus on empirical/quantitative fields)
- Mix of experience levels (but skew toward experienced reviewers)

## Suggested Methodology

### Phase 1: Workflow Deep Dive (n=8-10 reviewers)
**Format**: 45-60 minute one-on-one interviews
**Focus**: Understanding current state pain points and workflow

**Key Topics**:
- Walk through their last paper review from start to finish
- Identify specific friction points and time drains
- Current tools and workarounds
- Emotional state during different parts of the process

### Phase 2: Feature Validation (n=12-15 reviewers)
**Format**: 30-45 minute interviews with mockups/prototype
**Focus**: Testing specific feature concepts and value propositions

**Key Topics**:
- Reaction to intelligent highlighting concept
- Usefulness of multi-color mapping
- Preferred customization options
- Integration with existing workflow

### Phase 3: Message Testing (n=20-25 reviewers)
**Format**: Survey with follow-up interviews for subset
**Focus**: Quantitative validation of messaging and pricing

**Key Topics**:
- A/B test headline messages
- Willingness to pay at different price points
- Feature priority ranking
- Competitive alternatives consideration

## Expected Deliverables

### Research Report Including:
1. **Pain Point Analysis**: Detailed breakdown of current workflow friction points
2. **Feature Validation**: Ranking of proposed features by perceived value
3. **Messaging Recommendations**: Optimal headline and positioning strategy
4. **Pricing Insights**: Willingness to pay analysis and pricing recommendations
5. **Go-to-Market Strategy**: Recommended approach for initial market entry

### Tactical Outputs:
